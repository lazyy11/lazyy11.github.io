IELTS Speaking Materials

This post is for collecting useful materials, ideas, and vocabulary for IELTS Speaking.

雅思口语-语料库 (AME)

1. A Memorable Event: The JJ Lin Concert

(一个你经历过的、印象深刻的积极事件)

Core Narrative:

"About a year and a half ago, I had one of the most memorable experiences of my life: I went to a JJ Lin concert in Nanning with a friend. It was the final stop of his 2023 tour, and since he’s a superstar in China, the tickets were incredibly in-demand—they basically sold out in seconds. I had missed the initial sale, so I thought I was out of luck.

But then, serendipity kicked in. I have a friend who goes to college in Changsha, and I noticed from his social media that he’s a huge concert-goer. On a whim, I messaged him and asked if he was going to the Nanning show. It turned out he was, and he had managed to snag two tickets from a resale platform called Xianyu. He’d bought a 620 RMB ticket and a 920 RMB one, paying a premium of about 200 RMB for each, which is common for scalped tickets. His original plan was to use the one with the better view and sell the other.

This was my chance! I asked if I could buy the other ticket and we could go together, and he agreed. The only problem was that tickets with different price points are in completely different sections. We really wanted to experience the concert together, so my friend came up with a brilliant, if not long-shot, idea. He posted on Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book), another social media platform, asking if anyone had a seat adjacent to either of his two tickets and would be willing to trade.

You wouldn’t believe the odds. Out of about 30,000 people in the stadium, someone with a seat right next to our 620 RMB ticket saw his post. And she was looking to upgrade to a 920 RMB seat because it was closer to the stage! We struck a deal. On the day of the concert, we met her at a designated subway station and swapped the tickets. She paid my friend the price difference, which was around 300 RMB if I recall correctly.

We got into the stadium about an hour early, and another funny thing happened. A couple of girls sitting near us had a Polaroid camera and asked if we could help them take a picture. In return, they offered to take one of me and my friend and let us keep it. I snapped a photo of that Polaroid with my phone, and my friend kept the physical copy.

The concert itself was phenomenal. JJ Lin’s live performance is no joke; he truly is a ‘walking CD,’ just like people say online. His vocal prowess is just incredible. We were singing along the whole time, taking tons of videos and pictures. My phone almost died halfway through, but luckily, my friend had brought a power bank to save the day.

Looking back, while the concert was amazing, what made it truly special was sharing that experience with a friend. Honestly, going to a concert alone just wouldn’t have the same magic. It’s the company that counts."

Key Vocabulary & Phrases:

  • In-demand: 非常紧俏的
  • Sold out in seconds: 秒光
  • Out of luck: 运气不好
  • Serendipity kicked in: 巧合/缘分出现了
  • On a whim: 心血来潮
  • Snagged two tickets: 抢到了两张票
  • Resale platform: 二手平台
  • Paying a premium / Scalped tickets: 付了溢价 / 黄牛票
  • A long-shot idea: 希望渺茫的主意
  • Adjacent to: 与…相邻
  • Struck a deal: 达成协议
  • Vocal prowess / Pitch-perfect: 演唱技巧高超 / 音准完美
  • A ‘walking CD’: 行走的CD (a huge compliment for a singer’s live performance)
  • Save the day: 力挽狂澜，解救危局
  • It’s the company that counts: 有人陪伴才是最重要的

替代表达（分难度）：

  • 心血来潮（On a whim）→ 基础: suddenly/just decided; 中等: on the spur of the moment; 高级: on a whim
  • 运气不好（Out of luck）→ 基础: unlucky; 中等: didn’t have any luck; 高级: was out of luck
  • 抢到票（Snag tickets）→ 基础: get/buy tickets; 中等: manage to get tickets; 高级: snag tickets
  • 溢价/黄牛票（Pay a premium/Scalped tickets）→ 基础: pay extra; 中等: pay above face value; 高级: pay a premium/buy scalped tickets
  • 希望渺茫（Long-shot idea）→ 基础: unlikely idea; 中等: a bit of a stretch; 高级: a long-shot idea
  • 达成交易（Struck a deal）→ 基础: made an agreement; 中等: reached a deal; 高级: struck a deal
  • 行走的CD（Walking CD）→ 基础: he sang really well; 中等: he sounded just like the record; 高级: he was a “walking CD,” pitch‑perfect live

2. An Important Person: My Grandfather and His Erhu

(一个你亲近或尊敬的人)

Core Narrative:

"The person I respect the most is my grandfather. He’s a man of few words, very reserved, but he has a lifelong passion: playing the erhu.

I have some vague memories of living with him in the countryside as a toddler, but my most vivid memories are from my senior year of high school. I had moved out of the school dorm into a small apartment my dad rented for me nearby, and my grandpa came to stay with me to take care of my meals. Every day, when I came home from school for lunch or in the evening, I’d be greeted by the sound of his erhu music drifting from the apartment. He would be there, playing his instrument while waiting for me.

His skill is extraordinary; people who hear him play say he has the touch of a master with forty or fifty years of dedication. After I graduated, during that summer break, I decided I wanted to learn from him. Watching him, it looked so deceptively effortless. But when I actually tried my hand at it, I realized how incredibly daunting it was. I couldn’t stick with it for more than a few days and eventually threw in the towel. It really put his decades of perseverance into perspective for me.

Now, my grandfather is retired and lives back in the countryside. But some things never change. Whenever I go to visit him, I can often hear the beautiful, melancholic sound of his erhu before I even step through the door. That sound, for me, is the sound of home and a lifetime of quiet dedication."

Key Vocabulary & Phrases:

  • A man of few words: 一个沉默寡言的人
  • Reserved: 内敛的，不苟言笑的
  • Lifelong passion: 一生的热爱
  • Vivid memories: 清晰的记忆
  • Deceptively effortless: 看起来毫不费力（但其实很难）
  • Tried my hand at it: 亲手尝试了一下
  • Incredibly daunting: 极其困难，令人望而生畏
  • Threw in the towel: 放弃了 (an idiom from boxing)
  • Perseverance / Dedication: 毅力 / 专注，奉献
  • Put things into perspective: 让我对事情有了更全面的看法
  • Melancholic sound: 忧郁而优美的声音

替代表达（分难度）：

  • 内敛（Reserved）→ 基础: quiet; 中等: reserved; 高级: emotionally restrained
  • 一生所爱（Lifelong passion）→ 基础: something he really loves; 中等: a lifelong passion; 高级: a lifelong calling
  • 看似轻松（Deceptively effortless）→ 基础: looks easy; 中等: seems effortless; 高级: deceptively effortless
  • 放弃（Throw in the towel）→ 基础: give up; 中等: quit; 高级: throw in the towel
  • 令人望而生畏（Daunting）→ 基础: very hard; 中等: challenging; 高级: daunting
  • 忧郁的声音（Melancholic sound）→ 基础: sad sound; 中等: melancholic sound; 高级: a poignantly melancholic tone

3. A Familiar Place: My Dorm Room

(一个你熟悉的地点)

Core Narrative:

"The place I’m most familiar with right now has to be my dorm room. It’s a five-person room on the second floor, with a somewhat unusual layout. It’s not the typical design with a desk under each loft bed. Instead, as you walk down the main aisle, there are two sets of bunk beds on the left. On the right, there’s a single loft bed, and underneath it are the closets for all five of us. Next to the closets is a long, connected desk area where we all study.

The aisle leads to a balcony, which has the bathroom on the left and the sink on the right. We hang our laundry on two steel pipes running across the top, and the whole balcony is enclosed by a security grille.

My four roommates are great; three are from Guangxi province and one is from Qinghai. I’ve noticed a fascinating cultural habit from my Guangxi roommates: they don’t use mattresses. They sleep directly on a bamboo mat placed on the wooden bed board. I actually tried it once when my mattress was drying. At first, it felt incredibly hard and my back was sore. But by the second night, I started to acclimate, and after a week, it felt surprisingly normal.

Our dorm building is located in the northernmost, most secluded part of our massive campus. Our campus is huge, spanning about two or three kilometers from south to north, and my academic department is all the way on the south side. So, it’s a good ten-minute bike ride just to get to class. The upside is that our area is exceptionally quiet, which I appreciate because I’m a bit of a homebody.

The only real downside is the lack of a washing machine in our room. For my first two years, I washed all my clothes by hand. But in my junior year, I got lazy. A public laundry room opened on the first floor, and it’s super cheap—only 1.2 RMB per load. So now, I use the machines for my clothes and pants, though I still hand-wash my socks and underwear. I briefly considered buying one of those mini-washers for small items, but after browsing online, every single product had terrible reviews, so I just gave up on that idea."

Key Vocabulary & Phrases:

  • Unusual layout: 不寻常的布局
  • Bunk beds: 上下铺
  • Aisle: 过道
  • Security grille: 防盗网
  • Acclimate / Get used to it: 适应
  • Secluded / Remote: 偏僻的
  • A homebody: 一个宅家的人
  • The upside / The downside (or perk / drawback): 好处 / 坏处
  • Junior year: 大三
  • Gave up on that idea: 放弃了那个想法

替代表达（分难度）：

  • 不寻常布局（Unusual layout）→ 基础: special layout; 中等: unusual layout; 高级: an unconventional layout
  • 适应（Acclimate）→ 基础: get used to; 中等: adapt; 高级: acclimate
  • 偏僻（Secluded）→ 基础: far/quiet; 中等: remote; 高级: secluded
  • 宅（Homebody）→ 基础: stay at home a lot; 中等: a homebody; 高级: an introverted homebody
  • 好处/坏处（Upside/Downside）→ 基础: good/bad thing; 中等: upside/downside; 高级: perk/drawback
  • 放弃想法（Give up on that idea）→ 基础: stop the idea; 中等: drop the idea; 高级: give up on that idea

4. A Meaningful Object: My Kindle

(一件你常用或对你有特殊意义的物品)

Core Narrative:

"An object that’s become a big part of my life is my Kindle. My mom bought it for me right after I graduated from high school. Initially, my ambition was to use it to read classic literature, but I found I just couldn’t focus on them. Instead, I discovered the world of web novels, and I’ve been hooked ever since.

For me, the Kindle’s main advantage over a smartphone is that the e-ink screen is much easier on the eyes; it really mimics the experience of reading a physical book. It occupies a middle ground in terms of portability. It’s obviously more portable than a stack of books, but not quite as convenient as a phone that’s always in your pocket.

So, my habit has become situational. If I’m sitting at my desk in my dorm or at home, I prefer using the Kindle for a more immersive reading session. But when I’m lying in bed, I’ll admit, reaching for my phone is just more convenient."

Key Vocabulary & Phrases:

  • Ambition: 雄心壮志
  • Classic literature: 经典文学
  • I’ve been hooked ever since: 从那时起我就上瘾了
  • Easier on the eyes: 对眼睛更友好
  • Mimics the experience: 模仿…的体验
  • It occupies a middle ground: 它处于一个中间地带
  • Portability: 便携性
  • Situational: 看情况的，视具体情形而定的
  • Immersive reading session: 沉浸式的阅读

替代表达（分难度）：

  • 雄心（Ambition）→ 基础: plan; 中等: goal/ambition; 高级: lofty ambition
  • 上瘾（Hooked）→ 基础: really like it; 中等: got hooked; 高级: became utterly hooked
  • 护眼（Easier on the eyes）→ 基础: comfortable to read; 中等: easier on the eyes; 高级: gentler on the eyes
  • 中间地带（Middle ground）→ 基础: in between; 中等: middle ground; 高级: a sweet spot between … and …
  • 便携性（Portability）→ 基础: easy to carry; 中等: portability; 高级: carry‑around convenience
  • 沉浸式阅读（Immersive reading session）→ 基础: read deeply; 中等: immersive reading; 高级: a fully immersive reading session

5. A Hobby: Reading Web Novels

(一个你日常参与或有兴趣的活动/爱好)

Core Narrative:

"My absolute favorite hobby is reading web novels. I used to download them to my Kindle, but eventually, the convenience of my phone won out. Now, reading on my phone is a daily ritual. It’s especially a part of my bedtime routine—I honestly find it hard to fall asleep without reading a bit first. My routine is to turn off the lights, switch my phone to dark mode—with white text on a black background—open the ‘Tomato Novel’ app, and just get lost in a story. Before I know it, I feel drowsy, put my phone down, and I’m out like a light.

My taste in genres has become pretty eclectic, but it wasn’t always that way. I started out exclusively reading Xuanhuan and Xianxia, which are types of Chinese fantasy and martial arts fiction. The very first web novel that got me hooked was called ‘Battle Through the Heavens’. For web novel fans, it’s a classic. It pioneered a lot of popular tropes, like the ‘fallen genius’ plotline, where a prodigy suddenly loses his powers, only to regain them and become even stronger. It also popularized the ‘revenge against the fiancée’ trope, where the downtrodden hero gets his triumphant comeback against a fiancée who scorned him.

I read it during a very specific and intense time: the winter break when China abruptly ended its COVID lockdown policies. It caught everyone off guard. I, like most people, got infected. I had a fever for two days and a severe sore throat that lasted for over a week—it honestly felt like swallowing razor blades. Reading ‘Battle Through the Heavens’ during that period was the perfect escape. For a newcomer like me, its addictive, fast-paced plot was like a drug. It was a huge source of comfort and distraction when I was feeling sick and stuck at home."

Key Vocabulary & Phrases:

  • Daily ritual: 每天的惯例/仪式
  • Bedtime routine: 睡前程序
  • I’m out like a light: (俚语) 我一下子就睡着了
  • Eclectic taste: 广泛的品味
  • Pioneered a lot of popular tropes: 开创了很多流行的套路
  • Prodigy: 天才
  • Triumphant comeback: 辉煌的逆袭
  • Abruptly ended: 突然结束
  • Caught everyone off guard: 让所有人都措手不及
  • Felt like swallowing razor blades: 感觉像吞刀片
  • The perfect escape: 完美的精神寄托/逃避现实的方式
  • A huge source of comfort and distraction: 极大的安慰和消遣来源

替代表达（分难度）：

  • 每日仪式（Daily ritual）→ 基础: do it every day; 中等: daily habit/ritual; 高级: a daily ritual I can’t skip
  • 一下子睡着（Out like a light）→ 基础: fall asleep quickly; 中等: doze off fast; 高级: I’m out like a light
  • 开创套路（Pioneered tropes）→ 基础: started many common story ideas; 中等: pioneered a lot of tropes; 高级: popularized many genre‑defining tropes
  • 天才（Prodigy）→ 基础: very talented person; 中等: a prodigy; 高级: a gifted prodigy
  • 辉煌逆袭（Triumphant comeback）→ 基础: big comeback; 中等: triumphant comeback; 高级: a spectacular comeback
  • 措手不及（Caught off guard）→ 基础: surprised everyone; 中等: caught everyone off guard; 高级: blindsided the public
  • 刀片嗓（Swallowing razor blades）→ 基础: very sore throat; 中等: felt like swallowing razor blades; 高级: my throat felt razor‑raw
  • 完美解脱（Perfect escape）→ 基础: great way to relax; 中等: the perfect escape; 高级: an ideal mental escape hatch
